Corey's & Russell's Choice: The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt

The Gist

The third entry in the Witcher video game series, based upon the book series of the same name. The final outing of legendary witcher Geralt of Rivia embarks through a grim fantasy world where atrocity is commonplace, monsters are real, morality dubious, in search of his daughter. All the while seeking to evade the calamity that is the Wild Hunt.

Highlights

  • Open world RPG with a massive map to explore.
  • Rich story with multiple endings based on your choices.
  • Morally complext characters and scenarios.
  • Expansive side quests that add depth to the world.

Lowlights

  • Some technical issues on release, though many were patched.
  • Combat can be challenging for newcomers to the genre.
  • Large amount of content can be overwhelming for some players.

Why I love this game

Witcher 3 is the total package. It is beautiful, mature, and complex. It makes most standard fantasy look embarrassing by comparison.

Combat is fun an engaging and whilst it has less of a learning curved than the second game, it is still challenging enough.

The sidequests are also rewarding and well thought out, not just meticulous chores and though the main quest can lag at times, it is definately a high point of the game as well.

You also get a lot of bang for your buck. The base game is huge with no shortage of thing to do. DLC adds even more and all of it very fairly priced.
